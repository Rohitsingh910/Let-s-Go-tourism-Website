Best Tour Destination of India

Agra

Agra is home to the beautiful Taj Mahal. On your visit to Agra, also visit Agra Fort, Fatehpur Sikri and Sikandra.

Alleppey

Alleppey is the hub for backwater tourism in Kerala. House boats locally called “kettuvallam” are available on hire on a daily basis.

Amritsar

Amritsar, Punjab’s biggest city was the center of celebrations of the 400th anniversary (Parkash Utsav) of the installation of the Guru Granth Sahib.

Auli

Auli is a part of the Garhwal mountain range. It lies about 15 km from Joshmath on the Badrinath Pilgrimage route just beyond Rishikesh.

Bikaner

Bikaner is a prominent city of Rajasthan and is in the heart of the Thar Desert. It is famed for its architectural excellence.

Chattisgarh

Chattisgarh is the state in Central India, endowed with a rich cultural heritage and attractive natural diversity. It is also known as the land of waterfalls.

Chennai

Chennai, the first city of the south, and capital of Tamil Nadu, widely known as Madras till 1996, has a rich tradition and civilisation.

Chittorgarh

Lying on the Berach River, Chittaurgarh is a city and municipality in Rajasthan state of western India.

Corbett National Park

The oldest national park in India, established in 1936. Located in the Nainital district of Uttarakhand, it is India’s first national park.

Darjeeling

Explore the enchanting beauty of Darjeeling with its breathtaking views, iconic toy train rides, lush tea gardens, and vibrant local culture.

Delhi

The capital city of India, Delhi, can be labeled as a cultural melting pot where diverse Indian cultures dwell together.

Gangtok

Gangtok is the beautiful capital city of Sikkim. A Buddhist haven, Gangtok is wreathed with fluffy clouds and surrounded by majestic snow-capped mountains.

Goa

Goa is popular world-over for its beach trance parties, especially on the Anjuna beach. The beach is a major attraction for vacationers.

Gulmarg

A beautiful meadow nestled among the mountains of Kashmir, 50 km from Srinagar, at an altitude of 2730 meters.

Jaipur

Built by Raja Sawai Jai Singh II in 1727, Jaipur is known for its rich heritage and cultural significance, dazzling tourists.

Jaisalmer

In Rajasthan’s Thar Desert lies Jaisalmer, the golden city that transports you to the enchanting world of Arabian Nights.

Jodhpur

Known as the Blue City, Jodhpur is a blend of rich culture, history, and architectural marvels that are perfect for a cultural getaway.

Kalimpong

Kalimpong is a bustling town in West Bengal, known for its educational institutions, vibrant culture, and beautiful landscapes.

Katra

Katra is a town situated in the Trikuta Mountains of the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir. Starting point for Vaishno Devi.

Khajjiar

Khajjiar, known as the "Mini Switzerland of India," is a picturesque hill station in Himachal Pradesh.

Kolkata

Kolkata, the "City of Joy," is a vibrant blend of rich history, colonial architecture, cultural festivals, and a bustling artistic spirit.

Kovalam

A beach town by the Arabian Sea in Thiruvananthapuram city, Kerala, India. It is Kerala’s most famous tourist attraction, India’s most famous beach.

Lakshadweep

Lakshadweep is an Indian union territory with 36 islands in the Laccadive Sea in the Arabian Sea.

Leh

Leh is a District located in the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir. The largest town of Ladakh, Leh boasts of a nine-storey palace.

Madurai

Madurai is one of the oldest Indian cities with deep-rooted ancient heritage and culture.

Mahabalipuram

Mahabalipuram, located around 60 km away from Chennai in Tamil Nadu, is known as the City of Temples.

Manali

Explore Manali, India with our Manali Travel Tip and Guide. Plan your Manali and India vacations with India Travel.

Mandawa

Mandawa is a picturesque town occupying the heart of the Shekhawati region of Rajasthan. Visit this place and you will be reminded.

Mumbai

Mumbai is the capital city of the Indian state of Maharashtra, lying on the west coast of India and having a deep natural harbour.

Munnar

Munnar is the queen of hill stations located at the confluence of three mountain streams namely, Mudrapuzha, Nallathanni, and Kundala.

Orchha

A town in Tikamgarh district of Madhya Pradesh state, India. Orchha combines the architectural beauty of a medieval town.

Pangong Tso

Situated at a height of about 4,350 m, the 6 km long and 130 km wide Lake is bisected by the International border between China and India.

Pelling

A tiny but heavenly-beautiful town nestled in the interiors of Sikkim, Pelling is perhaps the second most popular destination in Sikkim.

Periyar

Titled as ‘God’s own country’, Kerala, best known for the beautiful backwaters, waterfalls, and rich flora and fauna attract visitors.

Pune

Pune has earned its name for its deep-rooted history, culture, and academic facilities. One of the names of the “Oxford of the East" in Pune is due to the presence of several distinguished educational and scientific institutions.

Ranthambore National Park

Famous for the iconic Ranthambore National park, the terrain of Ranthambore features a blend of lush forests and mighty hills.

Rishikesh

Rishikesh holds great spiritual and historical significance. Legend has it that after slaying Ravana, Lord Rama and his brother Lakshmana meditated here.

Srinagar

Situated in the Kashmir Valley, Srinagar is the summer capital of the Indian State of Jammu and Kashmir. Known as the “Paradise on Earth”.

Thanjavur

Thanjavur is popular for its centuries-old temples which are UNESCO World Heritage Monuments. The city is also famous for Tanjore paintings.

Udaipur

Snuggled up beside the tranquil Lake Pichola, Udaipur is a city which stands true to the heritage of being a royal counterpart of Rajasthan.

Varanasi

The land of Varanasi (Kashi) has been the ultimate pilgrimage spot for Hindus for ages. Often referred to as Benares, Varanasi is the oldest city.
